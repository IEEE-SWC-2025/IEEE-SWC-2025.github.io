Join us at the 2025 IEEE International Conference on Autonomous and Trusted Computing

With the rapid development of intelligent devices and information networks, innovations in perception and computation have become key drivers of social progress and technological innovation. In this era driven by intelligent technologies, the safety, reliability, and trustworthiness of automation systems are crucial for building a smarter, more efficient, and safer digital world. Autonomous and trustworthy computing technologies not only reshape the way we interact with machines, greatly enhancing the quality of daily life and work efficiency, but also drive the overall transformation and progress of society.

We welcome high-quality papers that describe original and unpublished research advancing the state of the art in autonomous and trusted computing. Topics for submissions include but are not limited to the following:

IEEE ATC 2025 Tracks and Topics

Track 1: Intelligent and connected vehicles
  • Environment perception and modeling
  • Localization and map building
  • Path planning and decision-making
  • Motion control
  • Smart sensors
  • Signal processing, sensor fusion, etc.
  • AI for vehicles
  • V2X, smart communication, etc.
Track 2: Artificial intelligence and LLM
  • Pattern recognition and image processing
  • Efficient fine-tuning and adaptation methods
  • Multimodal AI and cross-modal learning
  • Knowledge representation and reasoning
  • LLM and its applications
  • AI ethics, safety, and interpretability
  • AI for healthcare, AI for education, AI for finance, etc.
  • Edge AI and distributed intelligence
Track 3: Smart data fusion and target tracking
  • Multi-sensor fusion techniques and architectures
  • Bayesian filtering and probabilistic inference
  • Deep learning for data association and tracking
  • Distributed and cooperative tracking algorithms
  • Robust state estimation under uncertainty
  • Multi-object tracking and behavior prediction
  • Sensor calibration and synchronization
  • Real-time processing and edge computing for tracking
Track 4: Intelligent control and system
  • Industrial system modeling and optimization
  • Adaptive and robust control techniques
  • Decentralized and resilient control for DC microgrids
  • AI-driven control and reinforcement learning
  • Networked and distributed control systems
  • Fault detection, diagnosis, and self-healing systems
  • Intelligent control for robotics and autonomous systems
  • Cyber-physical systems and digital twins

Paper Submission

All papers need to be submitted electronically through the conference submission website (EDAS) in PDF format at EDAS. Each paper is limited to 8 pages (or 10 pages with over-length charge) for a regular full paper, 6 pages (or 8 pages with over-length charge) for a full paper including figures and references using the IEEE Computer Society Proceedings manuscripts style.

Paper Publication

Papers accepted by IEEE ATC 2025 conference will be published by the IEEE Computer Society Press. At least one author of each accepted paper is required to register and present their work at the conference; otherwise, the paper will not be included in the proceedings. All accepted papers will be submitted to IEEE Xplore and EI. Distinguished papers presented at the conference, after further revision, will be invited for submission to a special issue
